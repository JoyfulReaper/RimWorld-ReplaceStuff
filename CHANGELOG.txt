6/9/2026: Version 0.0.27
Milestone 1: Replacement System Refactor

This update introduces a major internal overhaul of Replace Stuff's replacement pipeline. While most of the work is behind the scenes, it lays the foundation for improved compatibility, maintainability, and future features.

Added:
-New replacement pipeline architecture.
-Improved state preservation framework for building replacements.
-Better handling of replacement initialization and building setup.
-Expanded debugging and diagnostic logging for development builds.

Changed
-Refactored ReplacementFrame logic into smaller, more maintainable stages.
-Separated persistent building data (storage settings, bills, temperatures, etc.) from transient state (stored inventory).
-Simplified instant replacement and standard replacement workflows.
-Improved rotation and spawn ordering during replacements.
-Centralized replacement initialization to reduce duplicate logic.
-General code cleanup and internal optimization.

Fixed
-Various issues related to replacement ordering and object lifecycle.
-Improved handling of replacement failures and edge cases.
-Multiple internal cleanup and stability improvements.

Known Issues
-Some storage-related edge cases, particularly with certain shelf configurations, are still being investigated.
-Additional state restoration features will continue to be refined in future updates.

Developer Notes

This milestone represents a substantial rewrite of the core replacement system. Although many of the changes are internal, they provide a cleaner foundation for future bug fixes, compatibility improvements, and new functionality.
